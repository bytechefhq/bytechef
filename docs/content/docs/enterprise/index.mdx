ByteChef Enterprise is the production-hardened distribution of ByteChef: **one platform for AI agents and the workflow automation that grounds them.** Reliable agents are a durable-execution problem, not a prompting problem — and ByteChef's workflow engine (suspend/resume, typed task state, deterministic replay, observability) was built years before the agent hype landed. Enterprise wraps that engine with the governance, scale, and white-label surfaces an enterprise rollout demands.

## What "unifies AI agents and workflow automation" means in practice

- **Agents are first-class workflow citizens.** Cluster elements plug LLMs, tools, RAG, and memory into the same suspend/resume engine that runs the rest of your workflows. No separate agent runtime, no separate observability stack.
- **Workflows are first-class agent tools.** Any workflow you've published becomes a callable tool over [MCP](/enterprise/extensibility/mcp-integration) — exposing your real business operations to Claude, Cursor, Copilot, or your own LLM scaffolding.
- **200+ components, one connection model.** The same Salesforce/Slack/Postgres/Stripe integrations your workflows use, your agents use. No re-implementing tool calls.
- **A real [knowledge base](/enterprise/data-knowledge/knowledge-base) on infrastructure you already run.** RAG on Postgres + pgvector, with [bring-your-own-embedding-model](/enterprise/data-knowledge/embedding-models). No separate vector DB to operate.

## Who Enterprise is for

- **AI platform teams** building production agents that have to act against real systems — and need durability, audit trails, and per-tenant guardrails their LangChain prototype didn't ship with.
- **Platform teams** standardizing automation across many tenants, environments, and business units.
- **Product teams** embedding AI-powered workflows and integration surfaces inside their own SaaS — a true white-label runtime for B2B2B.
- **Security and compliance teams** that need audit trails, RBAC, encrypted credentials, and a vault-backed secret story.
- **SREs and infra teams** running distributed deployments with their own brokers, schedulers, observability stacks, and storage backends.

## Community vs. Enterprise

ByteChef Community is the open-source core: the workflow engine, the agent primitives, the 200+ components, the UI shell, and the visible source code. Enterprise adds the governance, scale, and embedded-iPaaS capabilities above as a separately distributed binary, wired in at compile time via conditional configuration. Anything you build on Community runs unchanged on Enterprise.

See [License-gated EE distribution](/enterprise/governance-security/license-gated-distribution) for how the two binaries differ on disk and at runtime.
