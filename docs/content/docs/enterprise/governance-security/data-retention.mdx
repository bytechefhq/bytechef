Compliance regimes rarely ask "can you keep this data?" — they ask "can you prove it's *gone*?" Data Retention gives organization admins one place to set how long each class of ByteChef data lives before a scheduled cleanup job deletes it for good.

## One policy, organization-wide

Retention is configured at the **organization** level and applies uniformly to every workspace beneath it. There are no per-workspace overrides — a single, auditable policy is easier to defend in a compliance review than a patchwork of workspace-by-workspace settings.

Each category accepts a retention window from **1 day** to **5 years**, or **Forever** to disable automatic deletion.

## What you can set retention for

| Category | Covers | Configured today on |
|---|---|---|
| **Execution log retention** | Workflow execution records, step inputs/outputs, and the [cloud-native storage](/enterprise/scale-reliability/cloud-native-storage) artifacts they reference. | [Workflow executions](/enterprise/collaboration-devops/workflow-executions#retention) |
| **Soft-delete cleanup** | The recovery window for deleted projects, workflows, connections, data tables, and knowledge base documents before they are permanently purged. | — |
| **Audit log retention** | Privileged-action records in the [audit log](/enterprise/governance-security/audit-log#retention-and-export). | [Audit log](/enterprise/governance-security/audit-log#retention-and-export) |
| **AI conversation retention** | Copilot and AI Hub chat history, run history, and checkpoints. | — |

## How deletion runs

Deletion is **not instantaneous**. A scheduled cleanup job runs on a fixed cadence and removes records whose retention window has expired:

- Expired **execution logs** are deleted along with their storage artifacts, so storage cost tracks the retention policy rather than growing without bound.
- Once the **soft-delete** window closes, the resource is permanently removed and can no longer be recovered.
- **Audit records** past their window are deleted last — and only after they have been emitted to your [observability pipeline](/enterprise/governance-security/observability), so a SIEM that needs longer retention than ByteChef keeps its own copy.

## Defaults

Until you set a policy, every category defaults to **Forever** — ByteChef never deletes your data without an explicit retention decision. Set each category deliberately as part of your compliance onboarding.

## What gets logged

Every change to a retention window is itself captured in the [audit log](/enterprise/governance-security/audit-log) — the acting admin, the category, the previous window, and the new window — so "when did we shorten execution-log retention, and who approved it?" is always answerable.
