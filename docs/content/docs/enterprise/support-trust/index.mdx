The Support & Trust pillar is about the **non-technical** parts of "is this safe to run?" — service-level commitments, migration help when you're moving to or upgrading the platform, and source-available access so nothing is opaque to you.

## What you get

| Capability | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [Enterprise support SLAs](/enterprise/support-trust/support-slas) | Priority response, named contacts, migration assistance. |
| [Production migrations](/enterprise/support-trust/production-migrations) | Helm charts for monolith and microservices deployments; runbook-driven upgrade paths. |
| [Source-available code](/enterprise/support-trust/source-available-code) | Inspect, audit, and fork — no black-box vendor risk. |

## The principle

Production platforms succeed or fail on three things:

1. **Reliability** — does the thing work?
2. **Operability** — can your team run it?
3. **Trustability** — when something goes wrong, what's your recourse?

Reliability and operability are technical and covered in the rest of these docs. Trustability is what the Support & Trust pillar covers — and it's the thing that's hardest to add later.
