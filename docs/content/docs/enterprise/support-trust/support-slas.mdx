ByteChef Enterprise comes with service-level commitments that match production-grade infrastructure. The exact terms are part of your contract; the shape below is the standard offering.

## What's covered

| Element | Standard Enterprise |
|---|---|
| **Priority response** | Tiered response times by severity (P1/P2/P3/P4), measured from first contact. |
| **Named contacts** | A specific Customer Engineer and a Technical Account Manager assigned to your account, not a rotating queue. |
| **Engineering escalation** | A defined path from named contact to the engineering team that owns the affected subsystem. |
| **Migration assistance** | Hands-on help when you upgrade across major versions or change deployment topology. |
| **Architectural review** | Periodic reviews of your deployment with recommendations on scaling, security, and operational posture. |
| **24/7 P1 coverage** | Around-the-clock pager rotation for P1 incidents. |

## Severity definitions (typical)

| Severity | What it means | Typical response target |
|---|---|---|
| **P1** | Production down, no workaround. | Within 1 hour, 24/7. |
| **P2** | Production degraded or major feature broken; partial workaround exists. | Within 4 hours, business hours. |
| **P3** | Non-blocking bug or significant question. | 1 business day. |
| **P4** | Question, feature request, or minor issue. | 2 business days. |

Exact numbers are part of your contract — these are illustrative.

## What "priority response" actually means

The valuable part of "priority" is not just speed — it's that someone with context picks up. With a named contact:

- They know your deployment topology.
- They know the workflows that matter most to you.
- They know your runbooks.
- They can skip the discovery phase and go straight to root cause.

That cuts the resolution time for serious incidents by hours, not minutes.

## Channels

Standard support channels:

- **Email** — for P3/P4 and async.
- **Slack Connect / Teams** — for collaboration with your named contact.
- **Pager** — for P1 incidents.
- **Quarterly reviews** — scheduled with your TAM.

## What's not covered

Support covers the platform; it doesn't cover:

- **Your custom components.** ByteChef can review them; we don't own them.
- **Third-party API outages.** A Salesforce outage isn't a ByteChef incident, but we can help you build resilient retry/fallback logic in your workflows.
- **Workflow design.** Architectural review can help, but writing your team's workflows is your team's job. We provide patterns and best-practice templates.

## Migration assistance (specifically)

When you're moving from Community to Enterprise, upgrading across a major version, or changing topology (monolith → microservices, or single-region → multi-region), the named contact runs the migration with you:

- Reviews your current deployment.
- Writes the migration plan.
- Sits on the call during the cutover.
- Validates post-migration health.

See [Production migrations](/enterprise/support-trust/production-migrations) for the technical side.

## Engagement model

Standard cadence with your TAM:

- **Weekly or biweekly** sync during active deployment and migration phases.
- **Monthly** sync during steady-state operation.
- **Quarterly** architectural review.

Increase or decrease the cadence as your needs change.
